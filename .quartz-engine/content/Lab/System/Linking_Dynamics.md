## The Directive
We are shifting our copywriting and structural logic from **Linear** to **Networked**.
*   **Old Way**: Standard Markdown Links `[Link](path/to/file.md)` (The Web 2.0 Way)
*   **New Way**: WikiLinks `[[Link]]` (The Neural Way)

## Why `[[ ]]` is Functionally Superior

### 1. The Speed of Thought (Flow State)
*   **Scenario**: You are writing a script and reference "The Digital Garden".
*   **Standard**: You stop types. Go find the URL or file path. Type `[The Digital Garden](.../path/to/...)`. **Flow Broken.**
*   **WikiLink**: You type `[[Digi...` and hit Enter. **Flow Maintained.**
*   **Impact**: Copywriting speed increases by ~30% when you stop hunting for paths.

### 2. Bi-Directional Intelligence (Backlinks)
*   **Standard Links** are one-way streets. Page A points to Page B. Page B knows nothing about it.
*   **WikiLinks** create a conversation. If you link `[[Marketing]]` in 10 different strategy files, the `Marketing` note *automatically* lists all 10 references in its "Backlinks" section.
*   **Result**: We don't just write content; we build a **Knowledge Graph**.

### 3. Frictionless Refactoring
*   **Problem**: You move `Strategy.md` to a new folder.
*   **Standard**: All `[Link](old/path/Strategy.md)` links break. 404 Errors everywhere.
*   **WikiLink**: Obsidian *automatically updates* every `[[Strategy]]` link in the entire Vault.
*   **Result**: The system is anti-fragile. Move things freely.
